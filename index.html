The problem

Modern work skips the moment where judgment should happen.

Teams are not only dealing with bad AI answers. They are dealing with useful, fluent, persuasive outputs that move too quickly into business action.

Good work can still be unready work.

An AI generated ad recommendation may be useful. A vendor dashboard may be sophisticated. A client email may be beautifully written. But if the source is unclear, the claim is unsupported, the owner is missing, or the reviewer lane is wrong, the work is not ready to move.

Good Remedy gives the team a practical way to pause at the right point. Not to slow the business down, but to make the next move clearer, safer, less wasteful, and owned by the right human authority.

Case study pattern

Dear Sydney was not only an ad problem. It was a movement problem.

A public campaign can be polished, expensive, and technically impressive while still carrying the wrong use case into the wrong emotional setting. Good Remedy would not ask only whether the creative was fluent. We would ask what the ad was making acceptable, what claim it implied about AI, what human expression it displaced, and who should have held the movement before launch.

The correction is not generic caution. It is a Move Pack: identify the object, name the intended movement, separate claim from source, surface the human risk, route review, and define the next safer direction.

Boundary

Start small and do not overshare.

First review rule

Do not send confidential, regulated, identifying, legal, HR, clinical, financial, credential, or customer record material through the public form. Start with a redacted, synthetic, public source, or representative work story.
